Bacterial cell wall & antibiotics

Overview

This infographic illustrates the molecular mechanism of how β-lactam antibiotics inhibit bacterial cell wall synthesis in Staphylococcus aureus, ultimately leading to cell lysis through osmotic pressure.

This work aims to explain what antibiotics such as penicillin are, and how they attack bacterial cell wall synthesis.

Type Molecular visualization
Audience Lay audience with some science background
Tools Adobe Illustrator
Autodesk Maya
VMD, ChimeraX
Year 2025
Process
01

Research & Model Refinements

  • Penicillin-binding proteins (PBP) and S. aureus cell wall data were sourced from the Protein Data Bank (PDB). A specific PBP of S. aureus is selected based on UniProt documentation.
  • Antibiotic structures were obtained from PubChem.
  • Extra molecules, such as water and ions, were deleted to isolate the target structures. And missing structures (7 residues) of the PBP were added via prediction in ChimeraX, while VMD was used to adjust structural representations for clarity.

    • 02

    Ideation & Initial Sketches

    The workflow began with the creation of multiple thumbnails to explore composition.

    03

    Refined Sketch

    These initial ideas were developed into a single refined sketch. Asset list was created based on this draft.

    04

    Asset Creation

    3D models were textured and rendered in Autodesk Maya alongside 2D vector illustrations in Adobe Illustrator.

    05

    Final Layout

    The final composition and typography were assembled in Illustrator.
